About the Archive

Preserving memory, identity, and continuity

Who We Are

The Peepeekisis Cree Nation is a Treaty 4 Nation with deep roots in the lands, traditions, and histories of the northern plains.

This Virtual Archives & Museum was created to preserve photographs, belongings, and stories that document everyday life, ceremony, family, and community from the late 1800s through the mid-1900s.

Some materials are culturally sensitive and are therefore described rather than displayed. The archive reflects respect, responsibility, and care for cultural knowledge.

Purpose of the Virtual Museum

This project exists to support education, intergenerational learning, and cultural continuity. It is intended for community members, students, researchers, and visitors seeking to understand Peepeekisis history through authentic sources.

As the archive grows, additional sections will include oral histories, veterans’ stories, and community-contributed materials.
